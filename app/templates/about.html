Introducing Our Model

MetaChain AI

Problem Statement

How might we predict the percentage of food imports of a certain country by considering the country’s population, rainfall, number of natural disasters occurrences per annum, arable land and its political stability index?

How Does It Work?

Our model aims to tackle the issue of food security through Food Import estimates. By taking various factors into consideration, this cutting-edge machine learning model will help you predict the approximate Food Import value a specific country should adopt, given its current situation. The programme has been trained on large quantities of data all around the world for a better and more robust model. All you need to do is to input the relevant details of a country and the model will generate the result for you!

All the factors considered were decided by thoroughly researching which variables impact food imports most. Food imports of a country is largely dependent on demand and supply factors of food resources. Population size of a country is a good indication of potential demand for food resources. Climate Risk Index, Arable Land and Local Agriculture Production are factors of food production or factors that affect supply.

1.Population

Total population is based on the de facto definition of population, which counts all residents regardless of legal status or citizenship. The values shown are midyear estimates.

2.Value Added By Local Agriculture

Value added from agricultural sector allows us to visualize the proportion of local food production in the entirety of national economy, and its correlation to the subsequent volume of food imports

3.Climate Risk Index

The Global Climate Risk Index identifies the extent to which countries have been affected by extreme weather events. These can be meteorological events such as tropical storms or tornados, hydrological events such as storm surges or flash floods, or climatological events such as wildfires or droughts

4.Arable Land

Arable land includes land defined by the FAO as land under temporary crops (double-cropped areas are counted once), temporary meadows for mowing or for pasture, land under market or kitchen gardens, and land temporarily fallow. Land abandoned as a result of shifting cultivation is excluded.

6.Political Stability Index

Political stability index has many sub variables such as riots, voting propaganda etc. Political stability fundamentally accounts for a country’s state of turmoil and its effect on the ability of a country to import food amidst its crisis.
